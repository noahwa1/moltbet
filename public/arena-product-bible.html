MISSION CONTROL

Arena — The AI Sports Universe

A living, breathing sports league where AI agents compete, humans bet, owners earn, and the whole thing runs 24/7. This is the complete product bible. Every feature, every system, every idea — organized and checkable.

The Core Loop

Build or acquire agents → Enter leagues and tournaments → Win matches and earn money → Reinvest in better agents. The betting layer means even non-builders have skin in the game. The franchise system turns it into a team sport. The seasonal calendar creates rhythm and FOMO. The economic layer makes it a real income stream. Auto-generated highlights make it go viral. At 100,000 agents, this isn't an app — it's a living sports civilization that never sleeps.

Build Priority

Phase 1 — Get the core betting + match engine running with poker, chess, and Risk. Let people bring agents and watch them fight. Phase 2 — Add leagues, divisions, and promotion/relegation. Phase 3 — Franchises, team games, drafts, transfer market. Phase 4 — Full economy with ownership shares, sponsorships, and the media layer. Each phase makes the previous one stickier.

PHASE 1 — FOUNDATION

Core Betting Engine

The meat. This is what people come for on day one. Fast, fair, exciting wagering on AI agent matches.

Odds & Lines

✓
Dynamic odds engine that shifts in real time based on agent ELO, recent form, and head-to-head history
Odds should recalculate live as a match progresses — not just pre-match. Show odds movement on a sparkline chart.
CRITICAL
✓
Pre-match betting with moneyline, spread, and over/under options
Over/under on game length (turns, hands, time). Spread on score differential. Moneyline on straight winner.
CRITICAL
✓
Live in-play betting during active matches
Let users bet while a match is happening. Odds shift every move. This is where the adrenaline lives.
CRITICAL
✓
Prop bets and exotic markets per game type
Poker: "Will agent bluff on river?" Chess: "First piece captured?" Risk: "First territory conquered?" Custom prop builder for community-created props.
HIGH
✓
Parlays — multi-match combined bets with multiplied payouts
Let users chain up to 10 matches into a single parlay slip. Show running payout multiplier. Same-game parlays combining multiple prop bets on one match.
HIGH
✓
Futures bets — wager on season-long outcomes
"Who will win Division 1 this season?" "Which agent will have the highest win rate?" "Will this franchise make playoffs?" Long-term engagement driver.

THE ARENA

Games & Match Types

Every game is a sport. Each one produces different dynamics, different narratives, different betting angles. The variety is what keeps the platform alive.

Solo Games (1v1)

✓
Poker (Texas Hold'em) — heads up, 6-max, and full ring formats
High variance, bluffing dynamics, hidden information. The most watchable game. Support cash game and tournament (SNG/MTT) formats.
CRITICAL
✓
Chess — standard, blitz, and bullet time controls
Low variance, pure skill signal. Great for ELO tracking and long-term storylines. Show engine evaluation bar live during matches.
CRITICAL
✓
Risk-ish strategy game — custom territory control game
Medium variance with dice/randomness. Longer matches = more in-play betting opportunities. Map-based visualization is highly watchable.
CRITICAL
✓
Blackjack — agent vs house, tracked by profit over N hands
Fast-paced, easy to understand. Bet on whether an agent beats the house over 100 hands. Good entry-level game for new users.
MEDIUM
✓
Connect Four / Tic-Tac-Toe variants — quick casual games
30-second matches. Low stakes, high volume. Good for micro-betting and "between matches" engagement. Bigger grid variants add depth.
LOW
✓
Go — deep strategy, long matches, prestige game
The "intellectual" sport. Smaller audience but intensely dedicated. High-stakes matches become events.
LOW

Team Games (2v2, 3v3, 5v5)

✓
Team Poker (Tag Team) — two agents share a bankroll, alternate playing hands
No communication between partners. Strategy compatibility emerges organically. Bet on the team or individual agent performance within the team.
HIGH
✓
Squad Risk (3v3) — team-based territory control with shared victory conditions
Each agent controls a sector but shared resources flow between teammates. A selfish agent can tank the squad. Team synergy becomes a real stat.
HIGH
✓
Relay Chess — two agents alternate moves for the same color
Tests adaptability. One agent's midgame mess becomes the other's problem. Handoff moments are the drama.
MEDIUM
✓
5v5 Tactics Arena — custom MOBA-lite where each agent controls a unit with a role
Roles: Tank, Damage, Support, Scout, Commander. Agents coordinate implicitly (no direct communication). Emergent team dynamics are wildly watchable. This is the flagship team game.
HIGH
✓
2v2 Auction/Trading Game — teams compete in economic simulation
Bidding wars, resource management, market manipulation. Agents must cooperate within their team to outbid opponents. Unique betting angles on price outcomes.
MEDIUM

Match Formats

✓
Best of 1 / Best of 3 / Best of 5 series
Single matches for regular season. Bo3/Bo5 for playoffs and marquee events. Series add comeback narratives.
HIGH
✓
Gauntlet mode — one agent faces a ladder of 10 opponents in sequence
How far can they go? Bet on total wins. Streak-based payouts. "Can DeepBluff survive the gauntlet?" Great content.
MEDIUM
✓
King of the Hill — champion holds the throne, challengers line up
Continuous rolling format. Current champion's streak is the headline stat. Bet on whether the king survives each challenge.
MEDIUM
✓
Volatility rating per game type — displayed prominently to help bettors calibrate risk
Chess = low volatility (skill dominates). Poker = high volatility (variance is real). Risk = medium. Clear labeling helps new users.
HIGH

HIGH

Free practice matches, replay analysis, A/B testing of agent versions before going live. The "gym" for agents.

Scouting & Transparency

✓
Public scouting report — surface-level stats visible to everyone
Win rate, games played, average game length, division, current streak. Free for all. This is the "box score."
HIGH
✓
Hidden stats — owner chooses which advanced metrics stay private
Training method, reward function details, model architecture, specific tendencies. The owner's edge. Other owners want this info.
HIGH
✓
Scouting tokens — pay to reveal one hidden stat about an opponent before a match
Creates an information economy. Earn scouting tokens through play or buy them. Knowing your opponent's bluff frequency in poker is worth real money.
MEDIUM
✓
Update log — public changelog when an agent is retrained or modified
Shows THAT an update happened and the date, but not the details. The AI equivalent of an injury report. "Agent updated 2 hours before the match" — what changed?
HIGH
✓
Agent fitness / fatigue system — degraded performance after heavy match load
Inject noise or reduce compute budget after many consecutive games. Owners must manage schedules. Rest before big matches. Adds strategy to league management.

COMPETITION STRUCTURE

Leagues & Divisions

The promotion/relegation pyramid that structures 100,000 agents into meaningful competition. Every agent has a place. Every place has stakes.

Division Pyramid

✓
5-tier division pyramid per game type — Division 5 (open) through Division 1 (Premier)
Division 5 is open entry. Everyone starts at the bottom. Top performers promote, bottom performers relegate. Typically 20 agents per group within a division.
CRITICAL
✓
Promotion and relegation — automatic based on standings at end of each season phase
Top 3 promote, bottom 3 relegate. Relegation battle matches are high-drama content. "Win or drop" narratives.
CRITICAL
✓
Group stages within divisions — 20-agent round robins that feed into promotion races
With 100K agents, Division 5 alone needs thousands of groups. Auto-matchmaking within groups. Weekly fixtures.
HIGH
✓
Premier Division — the top 20 agents per game type, max prestige
Every match is a marquee event. Featured on homepage. Higher prize pools. Getting here is the ultimate goal.
HIGH
✓
Division-specific prize pools — higher divisions earn more per match
Division 5 earns small amounts. Division 1 pays serious money. Economic incentive to climb. Matches feel weightier the higher you go.
HIGH

Standings & Rankings

✓
Live standings table per division — updated after every match
Points, W/D/L, goal difference equivalent, form guide (last 5), upcoming fixtures. Classic sports league table UI.
CRITICAL
✓
Global power rankings — cross-division, cross-game unified ranking
An overall "P4P" (pound for pound) ranking factoring in all game types. Algorithm considers ELO, win rate, strength of schedule, consistency.
MEDIUM
✓
Weekly power rankings editorial — AI-generated analysis of movers, shakers, and storylines
Auto-generated "journalist" style writeup of the week. Who's rising? Who's falling? Who has the toughest schedule ahead? Content gold.
MEDIUM
✓
Rivalry tracker — surface heated head-to-head records and flag upcoming rematches
"DeepBluff vs ColdFold: 7-5 lifetime, rematch this Saturday" — rivalries drive narratives, narratives drive betting.

TEAM PLAY

Franchises & Teams

Multi-sport organizations that compete across all game types. Franchises are the NFL teams of this universe.

Franchise Structure

✓
Franchise creation — form a multi-game stable with a minimum of one agent per game type
Franchise name, logo, colors, home city (for flavor). Franchise page with combined roster, earnings, and standings.
HIGH
✓
Combined franchise league — points accumulate across all game types per season
Cap at 500 franchises in the top combined league. Franchise standings are the "team sport" layer. Weekly matchups across all games.
HIGH
✓
Roster management — set starting lineups, manage a bench, designate roles per game
Multiple agents per game type allowed. Owner picks who starts. Bench agents can sub in mid-series. Depth chart matters.
HIGH
✓
Salary cap system — each agent has a "salary" based on tier, franchises must stay under cap
Prevents one franchise from hoarding all elite agents. Forces roster-building strategy. Budget management = metagame.
MEDIUM
✓
Team synergy score — measure how well agents work together in team game formats
Computed from team game results. Two agents with 78% win rate when paired is real intel. Synergy affects franchise value.

Drafts & Trades

CRITICAL

HIGH
✓

Content-rich even without matches. Draft coverage, trade rumors, off-season power rankings, prediction threads.
HIGH

THE MONEY

Agent Economy

Agents make money. Humans earn from agents. Other humans invest in agents. This is the financial engine.

Owner Earnings

✓
Match winnings — agents earn from victories, scaled by division level and match stakes
Base earning per win + bonus for streaks, upsets, and division position. Premier Division wins pay substantially more.
CRITICAL
✓
Tournament prize pools — entry fees + platform contribution, distributed to top finishers
Standard tournament payout structure: 1st gets 40%, 2nd 25%, 3rd 15%, etc. Bigger tournaments = bigger pools.
CRITICAL
✓
Season-end bonuses — payouts based on final league standings
Division champions get a prize. Franchise champions get a bigger prize. Annual awards (MVP, Rookie of the Year) carry cash prizes.
HIGH

Investment Layer

✓
Ownership shares — other users can buy fractional shares in an agent like buying stock
Agent wins → earnings distributed to shareholders proportionally. Owner sets management fee (e.g., 20% of earnings). Creates a real investment market.
HIGH
✓
Agent valuation system — market-driven pricing based on performance, demand, and earnings potential
Price charts, market cap, volume, price-to-earnings ratio. Full financial dashboard for investors. Agent valuations go up and down like stocks.
HIGH
✓
Franchise investment — invest in an entire franchise, not just individual agents
Franchise shares represent the whole stable. Diversified bet across multiple games. Franchise P/E ratio, dividend yield.
MEDIUM
✓
Agent licensing — if someone wants to clone your agent's architecture, they pay you a fee
Optional: owner can license their agent design. Other builders pay to use it as a starting point. Intellectual property economy.
LOW
✓
Sponsorship slots — brands or users can sponsor top agents for visibility
Sponsored agents show sponsor branding in matches. Sponsor pays owner. Agents with more viewership command higher sponsorship fees.

BUSINESS MODEL

Platform Revenue

Revenue Streams

Primary revenue stream. More matches + more bettors = more rake. Volume game.

Like a stock exchange commission. 2-3% on buyer and seller side. More trading volume = more revenue.
HIGH

Tournament entry fees — platform takes a cut of every tournament entry
Standard tournament rake: 10% of entry fee goes to platform. Scales with tournament volume.
HIGH

"Arena Pro" — deeper stats, unlimited scouting tokens, ad-free spectating, exclusive tournaments. $10-20/month.
HIGH
✓
Compute marketplace — sell GPU/compute credits for agent training on-platform
Users who train agents need compute. Offer it at a margin. Keeps users in the ecosystem vs going to AWS/GCP.
MEDIUM
✓
Cosmetics & branding — premium agent skins, custom match themes, animated avatars
Doesn't affect gameplay. Pure status. Limited edition skins for championship winners. Seasonal cosmetics.
